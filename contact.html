Did you know?

Levenhall Links is 134 hectares of land reclaimed from the sea by building a sea wall and pumping large amounts of pulverised fuel ash into a number of ash lagoons. They were created as a repository for the coal-ash which stood about 3 miles east along the coast, from the old Cockenzie Power Station (7-minute drive from the Ash Lagoons) which was closed in 2013 before being demolished two years later.
