Welcome To East Lothian!

East Lothian's website of major visitor attractions.

Did you know? East Lothian is known as Scotland’s golf coast. Not only does the area have good weather, but it boasts some first class golf courses, including the current home of the Scottish Open.

LEVENHALL LINKS

What's it all about?

What is Levenhall Links? Well, from the reclaimed land by the seaside with a wall built and the numerous amounts of the ash from the ash lagoons, the Levenhall links is a coastal tourist attraction and an area full of refreshment and relaxation accessible to all that pass - meaning that it is free!. It is situated in Musselburgh, adjacent to, but also in between, the famous Musselburgh Racecourse and also the Prestongrange Industrial Museum. Its 134 hectares (331 acres) of land are filled with many visitor locations that range from the birdwatching point to the loch right in the centre of the Links.

How To Get There

Where can I find it?

As you pass the Racecourse, entering the village of Levenhall, you’ll notice the massive roundabout with a landmark of the life-sized golf ball representing the golf course of Musselburgh (made out of 439 horseshoes, with an image of a horse on it), and as you pass through Levenhall, you’ll also see the Levenhall Arms Pub and find that there is more in the distance - that is the Levenhall Links. The perfect place to park is along that same road you pass the pub on, Hope Place. You will then find the entrance once you turn in. Follow the footpath and carry on towards the view of the sea. At the final part of the path, head left onto the open green, AKA the Links. Continue and you’ll find yourself within the vicinity of the Lagoons and very shortly, you will have reached the core of the Links.

Getting Back to the Start

There is the Bird Reserve once you have arrived, on the right – the Ash lagoons (coal from the Cockenzie Power Station) adjacent to the birdwatching section is a tremendous provider for viewing the many flocks of birds that are situated there, a great activity and attraction for many visitors. After arrival, as you walk along the curved lake you will find the view of the sea wall. Head in that direction and you will see the coast of Prestonpans peeking out from the right and also spot a spectacular view of the Firth of Forth.

Continue left towards the distant view of Edinburgh and the Ash mounds. Follow the distant and long path you reach the loch again. Then turn right and walk across the green towards the barriers of the racecourse. Finally, turn left the way of the barriers towards the starting point.
